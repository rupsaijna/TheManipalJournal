Campus
5-Feb-2012, 3:17 PM
Manipal: The WelcomGroup School of Hotel Administration (WGSHA) organised an ‘up, close and personal’ session with Chef Vikas Khanna, a Michelin star chef and host of the second season of MasterChef India, for students, staff and the press at Chaitya Hall, Hotel Valley View on February 4. Khanna, an alumnus of WGSHA had attended the Annual Day celebrations of WGSHA the previous day and was awarded the ITC Chairman’s Distinguished Alumnus award.
Khanna spoke about how his journey to the south had been one of the longest and most memorable ones of his life. He said, “I was a very shy boy and coming to Manipal exposed
Khanna addresses the audience | Photo courtesy: Srija Kotagiri
me to the diversity of India. This university made me get over my fear of communicating.” He also added that Manipal University had given him a very important title, but more than that it had given him love and confidence.

Khanna also spoke extensively about his documentary series ‘Holy Kitchens’ and his exploration of the connection between spirituality and food. He told the captivated audience about the various people he had met on his journey through India in the search for different flavours for his book ‘My Great India’, which is yet to be published. He also touched upon his humanitarian work and his foundation South Asian Kid’s Infinite Vision (SAKIV), which raises funds and awareness for several charities worldwide.
